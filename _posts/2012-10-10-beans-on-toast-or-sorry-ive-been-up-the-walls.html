So to try out the new kitchen and my camera, I have documented my beans on toast method.

First put beans in to a pot and heat on the hob, (I never liked beans reheated in the microwave). Here I use Batchelors, but if you can't source them, I'm sure you can use another brand (I don't think I'll cope well as an ex-pat). I cook mine at 4 (out of 6) on my electric hob, so they'll be done in the lenght of time it takes to prepare the toast.

Then entoasten the breads. I usually toast for setting 2.5, then flip the bread over and do another 2.5, so it's nice and even.

Then, butter the bread. Use real butter, you can really taste the difference.

As I like to serve my beans on toast in a bowl, cutting the bread into little squares. In theory you can serve the beans on flat uncut slices, but then you need to eat them with a knife, and not just a fork.

Put the toast in a bowl, pour over the beans et voila! Beans on toast.

People do really fancy things with beans on toast, but sometimes, that's not what you want. That said, I would like to try to bake my own beans some time.
